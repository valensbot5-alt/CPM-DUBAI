CPM Machine Rental vs. Buying: Which Makes More Sense in Dubai?

By KneeCare Dubai Medical Team Last Updated: March 2026

When your orthopedic surgeon prescribes a Continuous Passive Motion machine after knee surgery, one of the first practical questions is whether to rent or buy one. In Dubai, where medical equipment can be expensive and storage space is often limited, this decision has significant financial implications. Having helped hundreds of patients across the UAE navigate this exact choice, we can share the data and experience to help you make the right call.

The short answer: renting a CPM machine is the better option for approximately 95% of patients. But the details matter, and there are specific scenarios where purchasing could make sense. Let us walk through the complete analysis.

Purchase Costs

The Break-Even Analysis

At AED 499 per week rental versus AED 10,000 purchase (mid-range), you would need to use the machine for approximately 20 weeks (5 months) before buying becomes cheaper than renting. Since the vast majority of patients use a CPM machine for only 4-6 weeks, rental saves you AED 7,000 to AED 12,000.

Key Insight: Even if you needed the machine for 8 weeks (longer than most protocols), rental at AED 3,992 still costs less than half the price of purchasing a mid-range machine. The math overwhelmingly favors rental for individual patients.

Advantages of Renting a CPM Machine

Professional Delivery and Setup

When you rent from a quality provider, the machine arrives at your home fully sanitized and ready to use. A trained technician sets it up, adjusts it to your leg length and initial range of motion settings as prescribed by your surgeon, and demonstrates proper operation. This eliminates the trial-and-error period that buyers face when setting up a machine themselves.

In our experience, proper initial setup accounts for a significant portion of patient satisfaction with CPM therapy. An improperly adjusted machine can cause discomfort, skin irritation, and even discouragement from using it consistently. For more on proper setup, see our CPM machine settings guide.

Ongoing Support

During your rental period, you have access to phone and WhatsApp support for any questions about the machine, settings adjustments, or troubleshooting. If a machine malfunctions (rare, but it happens), a replacement is delivered the same day. When you buy a machine, you are responsible for any technical issues, and service calls can take days.

Sanitization and Hygiene

Medical-grade CPM machines used by rental services undergo thorough sanitization between patients. This includes cleaning all contact surfaces, replacing pads and linings, and checking all mechanical components. When buying used machines, you have no guarantee of proper sanitization. This is particularly important for post-surgical patients whose immune systems are already working hard to heal.

No Storage Concerns

A CPM machine is not small. It is approximately 80 cm long, 40 cm wide, and 30 cm high, with a base frame that extends to accommodate different leg lengths. In Dubai apartments and villas where storage space can be at a premium, keeping a medical device you will never use again is impractical. Rental means the machine leaves your home the moment you are done with it.

Flexibility

Recovery timelines vary. Some patients achieve excellent range of motion in 3 weeks and can return the machine early. Others need 6-8 weeks. Rental allows you to extend or shorten your usage period based on actual recovery progress, not a guess made before surgery. You only pay for the weeks you actually need.

When Buying Might Make Sense

While rental is the right choice for the overwhelming majority, there are specific scenarios where purchasing could be justified:

Bilateral Knee Replacement (Both Knees)

If your surgeon has planned staged bilateral knee replacements (one knee now, the other in 3-6 months), your total usage period could reach 10-12 weeks across both surgeries. At that point, the cost difference narrows, though rental still often comes out ahead when you factor in storage, maintenance, and the fact that you still will not need the machine after both recoveries are complete.

Multiple Family Members

In large families where multiple members are scheduled for knee surgery within a 12-month period, shared purchase can be cost-effective. However, proper sanitization between users is essential, and you would need to arrange this professionally.

Clinical or Facility Use

Physiotherapy clinics, hospitals, and rehabilitation centers that treat multiple patients per week may find purchasing more economical than ongoing rentals. However, even clinics should consider bulk rental agreements, which offer significant discounts and include maintenance and replacement coverage that purchasing does not. We work with several clinics across Dubai and Abu Dhabi on custom rental programs.

Hidden Costs of Buying a CPM Machine

The purchase price is just the beginning. Many patients who buy a CPM machine discover additional costs they did not anticipate:

  • Delivery and setup: Unless included in the purchase price, delivery in Dubai typically costs AED 200-400. Professional setup may be additional.
  • Replacement pads and linings: These wear items need periodic replacement, costing AED 200-500 per set.
  • Depreciation: A CPM machine loses approximately 40-50% of its value the moment it leaves the store. If you try to sell it after recovery, expect to get AED 3,000-5,000 for a machine you paid AED 10,000+ for.
  • Repair costs: If the motor, controller, or mechanical components fail outside warranty, repairs can cost AED 1,000-3,000.
  • Storage: Even in a garage or storage room, the machine takes up valuable space for years while you figure out what to do with it.
  • Disposal: When you eventually decide to get rid of it, disposing of medical equipment in the UAE requires proper channels.

For Clinics: Rental vs. Fleet Ownership

If you run a physiotherapy clinic or rehabilitation facility in Dubai, the calculation is different. Here are the factors to consider:

Fleet Ownership

  • Upfront investment of AED 40,000-75,000 for 5 machines
  • Ongoing maintenance and repair costs
  • Sanitization responsibility (staff time, cleaning supplies)
  • Machine depreciation and eventual replacement cycle
  • Storage when machines are not in use
  • Full control over availability

Bulk Rental Program

  • Discounted weekly rates for volume commitments
  • Maintenance and replacement handled by provider
  • Sanitization included
  • Scale up or down based on patient volume
  • No capital expenditure
  • Always access to latest machine models

In our experience working with clinics, the bulk rental model is increasingly popular because it converts a large capital expense into a predictable operating cost, and clinics never have to worry about machine maintenance or replacement.

Insurance Coverage in the UAE

A common question we receive is whether health insurance covers CPM machine rental in the UAE. The answer varies by provider and policy:

  • Premium plans (Daman Enhanced, Cigna Global, Bupa) often cover medical equipment rental as part of post-surgical rehabilitation. You will typically need a prescription from your orthopedic surgeon and pre-authorization.
  • Standard plans may cover a portion of the cost or require significant copayment.
  • Basic plans generally do not cover home medical equipment rental.

We recommend contacting your insurance provider before surgery to understand your coverage. We provide all necessary documentation, including detailed invoices and medical justification letters, to support insurance claims.

The Verdict: Rent in Almost Every Case

After helping hundreds of patients across Dubai, Abu Dhabi, Sharjah, and the wider UAE, our recommendation is clear: renting a CPM machine is the smarter choice for 95% of individual patients. The cost savings are substantial (typically AED 7,000-12,000), you get professional support throughout your recovery, and you avoid the hassle of storing and eventually disposing of a medical device you no longer need.

The only exceptions are clinics treating multiple patients or families with multiple upcoming knee surgeries within a short timeframe. Even in these cases, bulk rental programs often prove more practical and cost-effective than purchasing.
