## Opening (data-first, no generic intro)
Roughly 8 in 10 patients who use continuous passive motion (CPM) in the first two weeks after knee replacement reach 90° of flexion faster than those who rely on exercises alone. But the machine only helps if you actually use it 4 to 6 hours a day at home, and that depends entirely on one thing: whether the machine is comfortable and simple enough that you don't avoid it. This guide ranks what matters in a home CPM in 2026, and why, for most Dubai patients, the smart move is not buying at all.

## What a "home-use" CPM machine actually needs
A hospital CPM and a home CPM are different problems. At home, nobody adjusts it for you. So the priorities shift:
- **Range:** must reach 0° to 120° flexion (covers the full TKR/ACL recovery curve).
- **Quiet motor:** you'll sleep next to it · loud units get switched off.
- **Simple controls:** one dial for angle, one for speed. If it needs a manual, it's wrong for home.
- **Comfort cradle:** padded, adjustable to leg length. Pressure points end sessions early.
- **Safety stop:** patient-held remote that pauses instantly on pain.

## The 5 things that separate a good CPM from a frustrating one
1. **Warm-up mode** · eases into range instead of jerking to the set angle.
2. **Progressive angle memory** · remembers yesterday's max so you advance, not restart.
3. **Leg-length adjustment** · fits the patient, not the other way around.
4. **Weight** · under 12 kg so it can be moved between bed and sofa.
5. **Hygiene** · wipeable surfaces and replaceable pads (critical for rentals).

## Top CPM machine types for home recovery in 2026
Rather than chase brand names that change yearly, judge by category:
- **Premium clinical-grade knee CPM** · full 0-120° range, warm-up, memory, quiet. Best outcomes, highest price. Ideal as a *rental* because you get clinical quality without the cost.
- **Mid-range home CPM** · solid range, fewer comfort features. Fine for straightforward recoveries.
- **Budget/compact units** · limited range or noisy motors · often abandoned after a few days. False economy.

For post-surgical knee recovery, clinical-grade is the only category that reliably delivers the early range-of-motion gains surgeons want.

## How to choose the right settings on any machine
Whatever machine you use, the protocol matters more than the brand:
- Start at the range your surgeon set (often 0-40° day one).
- Increase flexion ~5-10° per day as comfort allows.
- 4 to 6 hours daily, split into sessions.
- Never push into sharp pain · use the remote stop.
